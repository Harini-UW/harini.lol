## What I learned that blew my mind

This might sound philosophical, but my favorite learning this month was simple:

> You don't need motivation. You need a mission.

If the goal is clear enough, motivation stops being something you chase, it just shows up. And this month felt like proof of that.

On the other hand, somewhere along the way, I think I finally wired my brain to think like a software engineer. System design, modular code, and building the software side of mechatronics systems finally make sense and I feel confident doing it.

Did I spend an unreasonable amount of time debugging?

Yes. Painfully yes.

But that's also where most of the learning happened. I genuinely think the best way to learn system design is to build a bad one first… and then fix it until it works. That process ended up being one of my biggest takeaways from this term.

## What I Built

**RAG for Portfolio** ([harini.bio](https://harini.bio))

I wanted my portfolio to be interactive and searchable in a natural way, so I built a RAG system on top of it. Now instead of scrolling through pages, you can just ask:
- *"Explain your entire technical journey using only three turning points."*
- *"Which experience outside engineering has most influenced how you approach technical problems?"*
- *"Which project would you demo if you had only 90 seconds, and how would you structure that demo?"*

**JARVIS — Personal Agent**

I built a personal AI agent that actually gets me. It's not just another chatbot; it's built to handle organizing tasks, daily briefings, tracking goals, managing my calendar, and keeping me from forgetting birthdays. I built a local web app with a voice agent and dashboard, but when I'm on the go, I just use Telegram.

## Who I met

One of the coolest parts of this internship was getting to coordinate with industry professionals across companies like Siemens, ABB, and Yaskawa.

Handling technical back-and-forth conversations with people who have 40+ years of experience as a first-year student felt surreal. But it also built a kind of confidence I didn't know I was capable of yet.
