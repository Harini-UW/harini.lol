I've wanted to write a newsletter for a long time. I've also talked myself out of it for a long time — who's going to read it, what would I even say, isn't this self-indulgent, don't I have actual work to do?

All of those thoughts were real. None of them were the real reason.

The real reason is that writing for an audience felt like a commitment I might not keep. And I'd rather not start than start and then quietly disappear. I've done that with enough things already.

But here's what changed: I stopped thinking of this as something I needed to be consistent about and started thinking of it as something I wanted to *try*. Those feel like the same thing but they're not. Consistency is a standard. Trying is a direction.

---

## Why a newsletter

I spend a lot of time thinking about things I never say out loud. Observations about how I spend my time, questions about whether I'm living the way I actually want to, small shifts I notice in myself between one month and the next.

A newsletter felt like a place to put that. Not as advice — I'm 19, I don't have advice — but as thinking out loud. The kind of thing where writing it helps me understand what I actually believe.

If it's useful to you too, that's a bonus. If not, that's fine. I'm mostly writing to figure things out.

---

## What this will be

Once a month. Something I noticed, something I read, something small that made a difference. No particular format beyond that.

I don't want to over-engineer it. I want it to feel like a letter.

---

Thank you for being here from the beginning. That means something to me.
