Maybe I didn’t start early. But I’m starting deliberately.

I’m curious about too many things, some niche, some utterly mainstream, but that curiosity is the constant. I love writing, reading, music, movies, and traveling. And somewhere between all of that, I’m trying to grow into the version of myself I put on LinkedIn: someone genuinely passionate about data science and design.


---

## What I learned that blew my mind


Working on agentic systems alongside a deep learning certification has fundamentally shifted how I approach problems. I used to think problem-solving was about arriving at the right answer; now, I see it’s about designing the right architecture, thinking like a consumer, structuring feedback loops, and deciding where autonomy lives. Intelligence, I’m realizing, is less about computation and all about system design.

That same systems perspective unexpectedly changed how I started looking at design and engineering. When I got into Formula 1 last year, I became fascinated with the engineering behind a 300 km/h zooming machine. Reading How to Build a Car by Adrian Newey made something click: nothing in a high-performance system exists in isolation. A rear wing adjustment isn’t just about downforce; it reshapes the entire aerodynamic ecosystem, from drag coefficients to tire degradation.

What fascinates me most is the obsession with marginal gains. Performance improvements are measured in hundredths of a second. It’s iterative, constraint-driven engineering at its best and it’s addictive.

## What I Built


I put these systems ideas to the test this month and took the win at my first coding hackathon with Mind Mirror. We built an AI chatbot constrained to ask only structured, thoughtful questions to support authentic brainstorming. Since then, I’ve been rebuilding our mind-mapping feature using interactive 3D layouts that spatially cluster ideas to reveal conceptual patterns.
