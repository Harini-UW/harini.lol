I’m curious about too many things, some niche, some utterly mainstream, but that curiosity is the constant. I love writing, reading, music, movies, and traveling. And somewhere between all of that, I’m trying to grow into the version of myself I put on LinkedIn: someone genuinely passionate about data science and design.

---

## What I learned that blew my mind

This month was very internship-focused, and I went deep into the data center space. With Alberta becoming a hub and moves like CoolIT's acquisition, it feels like the right place at the right time.

The biggest realization: 
We're no longer just designing electrical systems — we are designing electro-thermal systems. Cooling is tightly coupled with power, efficiency, and scalability, and technical advancements in this sector are going to be fascinating to watch.

On the other hand, human-in-the-loop development finally clicked for me. 
It's all about fast, iterative building driven by consistent, user experience-focused design. It's asking better questions, testing constantly, and letting curiosity guide what you build next.

---

## What I Built

What sparked that realization?

I finally built my [portfolio](https://harini-uw-portfolio.vercel.app/)!! 
It's a CLI/terminal-inspired experience where you navigate through `/` commands. There's a 3D avatar, some fun easter eggs, and of course… an F1 theme woven in. It genuinely didn't feel like work — I had fun building it and just wanted to keep adding to it.

At FigBuild 2026, we built [PRESENSE](https://devpost.com/software/presense-fgen5v), an intuition-powered safety app. 
The challenge was to design an imaginative sensory experience, and we focused on helping women recognize and act on their "spidey sense." One of those projects where the concept felt slightly crazy… but also kind of right. Biggest takeaway: optimizing user experience is everything.
